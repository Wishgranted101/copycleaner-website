Why ChatGPT Text Looks Fine Until You Paste It

ChatGPT displays text in a web interface designed for readability. When you copy that text, your browser grabs everything—including hidden formatting codes your eyes never see.

Your document editor (Google Docs, Word, Notion, email) tries to interpret those codes. Sometimes it works. Usually, it doesn't.

Here's what actually happens:

Smart quotes and special characters

AI tools default to curly quotes ("like these") instead of straight quotes. These look professional in ChatGPT but cause problems in code editors, plain text fields, and certain applications. You also get em-dashes, en-dashes, and non-breaking spaces that mess with formatting.

Multiple spaces and tabs

ChatGPT sometimes adds multiple spaces between words for visual formatting. Your document sees those as actual space characters. Now your sentence looks    like    this.

Broken line breaks

AI tools wrap text to fit the chat window. When you copy, those visual line breaks become real line breaks. Your paragraph turns into a
fragmented

Markdown leftovers

If ChatGPT used **bold** or *italics* in its response, you might get the actual markdown symbols in your paste. Sometimes it converts them. Sometimes it doesn't. It's inconsistent.

Invisible characters

Zero-width spaces, soft hyphens, and other Unicode ghosts hide in your text. You can't see them, but they're there. They break searches, mess up word counts, and cause mysterious formatting errors.

Why "Paste Without Formatting" Doesn't Actually Fix This

You've tried Cmd+Shift+V (Mac) or Ctrl+Shift+V (Windows). It's the standard workaround everyone suggests.

Here's what it does: removes bold, italics, font styles, and colors.

Here's what it doesn't do:

  • Fix extra spaces between words
  • Remove broken line breaks mid-sentence
  • Convert smart quotes to regular quotes
  • Delete invisible characters
  • Normalize paragraph spacing

So you still end up fixing it manually. Which defeats the purpose when you're trying to work fast.

You copy this:

The fix: CopyCleaner converts all curly quotes and apostrophes to straight quotes that work everywhere.

Best Practices When Using AI Text

Even with a cleaning tool, here's how to avoid formatting headaches:

Clean before you paste into final documents

Don't paste AI text directly into your email client, CMS, or Google Doc. Clean it first, then paste. Prevents formatting contamination.

Use plain text editors for drafts

If you're working on longer content, paste AI output into Notepad (Windows) or TextEdit (Mac) first. Plain text editors can't hide formatting problems. You'll see them immediately.

Set up a workflow

If you use ChatGPT constantly, bookmark CopyCleaner and make it step 2 of your process: (1) Generate in ChatGPT, (2) Clean in CopyCleaner, (3) Paste into final destination.

Be careful with code

CopyCleaner removes extra spaces and invisible characters, which is good for most code. But if your code relies on specific indentation (like Python), review it after cleaning to make sure spacing is preserved correctly.
